1st State (1744-1818)

Riyadh originated from the merging of ancient villages in central Najd including Mi'akal, al-Beneyah and Manfouhah. The new town became well known during the First Saudi State in the 17th century.

2nd State (1902-1932)

Riyadh's boundaries and special architectural character became more defined during the Second Saudi State, for which Riyadh served as capital.

3rd State (1932- Modern Day)

The golden age of the city of Riyadh began on the 15th January 1902 with the restoration of the city by King Abdulaziz and establishment of the Modern Saudi State.
